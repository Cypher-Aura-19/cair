Our Mission

Our Mission & Vision

CAIR Michigan exists to protect the civil rights of American Muslims, enhance understanding of Islam, promote justice, and empower our community to thrive as proud American Muslims.

Defend Our Community

Free legal representation for Muslims facing workplace discrimination, school bullying, hate crimes, FBI encounters, and religious accommodation denials.

Combat Islamophobia

We challenge anti-Muslim bias in media, politics, and public discourse—ensuring accurate representation of Islam and our community.

Empower American Muslims

Know Your Rights workshops, youth leadership programs, civic engagement training, and resources to help Muslims navigate life in America confidently.

Our Core Values

Rooted in Islamic Principles

Our work is guided by the Islamic values of justice, compassion, and service to humanity

Rahma (Compassion)

We serve every community member with mercy and understanding, recognizing the pain of discrimination and the courage it takes to seek help.

Adl (Justice)

We stand firm for justice regardless of who is affected—defending the rights of all, as commanded by our faith.

Ummah (Community)

We believe in the strength of our community united. When one Muslim is harmed, we all stand together in response.

Ihsan (Excellence)

We pursue excellence in everything we do—providing the highest quality legal advocacy and community service.

Our Journey

25+ Years of Impact

Key milestones in our history of defending civil rights

1998

CAIR Michigan Founded

Established as a chapter of the national CAIR organization to serve Michigan's Muslim community.

2001

Post-9/11 Response

Expanded services dramatically to address surge in discrimination and hate crimes against Muslims.

2010

Legal Department Expansion

Grew our legal team to handle increasing caseload and provide more comprehensive services.

2020

Digital Transformation

Launched virtual services and expanded online resources to reach more community members.

Today

Continuing the Mission

Serving thousands annually through legal aid, education, and advocacy programs.
