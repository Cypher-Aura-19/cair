Know Your Rights

Empowering American Muslims to understand and protect their constitutional rights

Right to Remain Silent

You have the right to remain silent and not answer questions from law enforcement.

Right to an Attorney

You have the right to speak with an attorney before answering any questions.

Right to Refuse Search

You can refuse consent to search your home, car, or belongings without a warrant.

Right to Due Process

You are entitled to fair treatment through the judicial system.

If Approached by Law Enforcement

Remember these key points to protect your rights

01

Stay Calm

Remain calm and polite. Don't argue or resist.

02

Ask Questions

Ask "Am I free to leave?" If yes, calmly walk away.

03

Request a Lawyer

Say "I want to speak to a lawyer" then remain silent.

04

Document Everything

Write down badge numbers and get witness information.

Common Scenarios for Muslims

Know your rights in situations that may affect American Muslims

Workplace Rights

You have the right to religious accommodation for prayer, hijab, and religious holidays. Employers must provide reasonable accommodations.

Airport & Travel

You have the right to remain silent. You can request a private screening. Document any profiling and contact CAIR.

FBI Interviews

You have the right to remain silent and request an attorney. You are not required to answer questions without a lawyer present.

Schools & Universities

Students have the right to religious expression, prayer, and accommodation for religious holidays. Report bullying or discrimination.
